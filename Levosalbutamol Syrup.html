Levosalbutamol Syrup

Indications:- asthma

SYRUP PRESENTATION:
Each 5ml Contains:
Levosalbutamol 1mg



Levosalbutamol is a short-acting β2 adrenergic receptor agonist used in the treatment of asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).Salbutamol has been used as a racemic mixture, although beta2-agonist activity resides almost exclusively in the (R)-enantiomer.The enantioselective disposition of salbutamol and the possibility that (S)-salbutamol has adverse effects have led to the development of an enantiomerically pure (R)-salbutamol formulation known as levosalbutamol.

  • Bronchospasm
  • Asthma
  • Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)

It acts by relaxing smooth muscle in the bronchial tubes to increase air flow and relieve acute shortness of breath. β2 adrenergic receptors on airway smooth muscle are Gs coupled and their activation by levosalbutamol leads to activation of adenylate cyclase and to an increase in the intracellular concentration of 3',5'-cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cyclic AMP). Increased cyclic AMP activates protein kinase A which itself inhibits the phosphorylation of myosin produces lower intracellular ionic calcium concentrations, inducing muscle relaxation. Increased cyclic AMP concentrations are also associated with the inhibition of the release of mediators from mast cells in the airways, potentially contributing to its benefit in asthma attacks.

Levosalbutamol is well absorbed from the gastrointestinal tract and have time to maximum drug concentration (tmax) values ranging from 45 to 360 minutes. Bioavailability of (R)-salbutamol increases from 9% after a single oral dose to 30% at steady state. Intestine is the main site of enantio-selective presystemic metabolism of salbutamol for drug absorbed in the gastrointestinal tract. It is mainly excreted in urine. Half-life of levosalbutamol is approximately 3.3–4 hours.

Common side effects may include:

  • Dizziness, nervousness, tremors;
  • Runny nose, sore throat;
  • Chest pain or tightness, irregular heartbeats;
  • Pain; or.
  • Vomiting.

This drug can interact with the following medications:

  • Short-Acting Bronchodilators
  • Beta-blockers
  • Diuretics
  • Digoxin
  • Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors or Tricyclic Antidepressants
  • Halogenated anaesthetics


The drug should not be used or can be used with high precautions in the following conditions:

  • Hypokalaemia
  • Diabetes mellitus 
  • Coronary insufficiency
  • Hypertension
  • Cardiac arrhythmias
  • Convulsive disorders
  • Hyperthyroidism

  • Do not exceed the recommended dose
  • Levosalbutamol can produce paradoxical bronchospasm, which may be life-threatening
  • Asthma may deteriorate acutely over a period of hours or chronically over several days or longer. If the patient needs more doses of levosalbutamol than usual, this may be a marker of destabilization of asthma and requires re-evaluation of the patient and treatment regimen, giving special consideration to the possible need for anti-inflammatory treatment, e.g. corticosteroids.
