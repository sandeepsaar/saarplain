Clotrimazole is an antifungal with broad-spectrum and is structurally derived from imidazole and triazole. Beclomethasone Dipropionate is a potent glucocorticoid steroid which is a prodrug is activated inside the cell.

Clotrimazole inhibits fungus growth by inhibiting the division. It alters the permeability of the fungal cell wall by inhibiting the biosynthesis of ergosterol and other sterols required for cell membrane production that eventually leads to cell death. It also inhibits the activity of enzymes within the cell.

Beclomethasone is a corticosteroid used to help relieve redness, swelling, itching, and other discomfort of fungus infections. It may work by increasing the transcription of genes coding for anti-inflammatory proteins, including lipocortin-1 and interleukin-10. Corticosteroids suppress the multiple inflammatory gene expression by promoting histone deacetylation, resulting in tighter coiling of DNA and reduced access of transcription factors to their binding sites.

Beclomethasone dipropionate can interact with: Acetaminophen; Chlorpheniramine; Dextromethorphan and Phenylephrine.

The product is possible safe for most adults. There is no disadvantage associated with the product if taken under the recommended usage however irritation, sensitivity, burning, acne and excessive drying can occur in some patients.

  • Avoid contact with your eyes, nose, mouth or any areas of broken skin.
  • Do not apply on cuts and wounds.


HOW TO USE:
