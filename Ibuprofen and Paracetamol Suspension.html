SUSPENSION PRESENTATION:
Each 5 ml suspension contains:

A pharmaceutical suspension is a coarse dispersion of insoluble solid particles in a liquid medium. Suspension are effective in dispensing of hydrophobic drugs, tastemasking of unpleasant ingredient, prevents degradation of drugs due to hydrolysis, oxidation or microbial activity; easy swallowing for young or elderly patients; and efficient intramuscular depot therapy. It provides patient compliance mainly to paediatric and geriatric. Ibuprofen & Paracetamol shows AAA action which is anti-inflammatory, analgesic and antipyretic action.

It is used for the treatment of:

  • Moderate to severe pain and fever associated with musculoskeletal system
  • Dysmennorhoea
  • During post-operative and post-traumatic period.

Ibuprofen and paracetamol is categorized as non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs which and shows their action by inhibiting COX enzyme responsible for production of prostaglandins as these prostaglandins are responsible for the symptoms like fever pain etc. Ibuprofen is propionic acid derivative and is reversible inhibitor of COX-1 and COX-2. It mainly exhibit anti-inflammatory and analgesic effect and provide relief from pain and inflammation. Paracetamol is chemically acetaminophen derivative also inhibits COX enzyme.

ABSORPTION: Ibuprofen is well orally absorbed Cmax can be attained within 1 to 2 hours. Paracetamol is also rapidly absorbed from GIT
VOLUME OF DISTRIBUTION: The apparent volume of distribution of ibuprofen is of 0.1 L/kg and for paracetamol is 1.29 l/kg.
METABOLISM: They both are metabolised in liver by CYP enzymes
ROUTE OF ELIMINATION: Metabolites of both are excreted in urine
Half-life of ibuprofen is 1.2-2 hours and of paracetamol is of 3.6 h
