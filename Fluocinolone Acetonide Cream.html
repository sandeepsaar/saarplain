Fluocinolone topical is used in treating itching, dryness, redness, crusting, inflammation, scaling, and discomfort of various skin conditions, including psoriasis and eczema . Fluocinolone belongs to the class of medications known as corticosteroids. It functions by activating natural substances in the skin reducing swelling, redness, and itching.

Fluocinolone Acetonide is indicated for relieving pruritic and inflammatory manifestations of corticosteroid-responsive dermatoses.

Fluocinolone acetonide shows its action by inhibiting edema, fibrin deposition, capillary dilation, leukocyte migration, capillary proliferation, fibroblast proliferation, collagen deposition, and scar formation. Fluocinolone Acetonide may have a high binding affinity for the glucocorticoid receptor. After binding the receptor, the receptor-ligand complex thus formed translocates itself into the cell nucleus and binds to many glucocorticoid response elements in the promoter region of the target genes. This effect promotes the induction of phospholipase A2 inhibitory proteins (lipocortins). Fluocinolone induces mainly one of the lipocortins, annexin 1, which will later mediate the synthesis of inflammatory mediators such as prostaglandins and leukotrienes by inhibiting the release of arachidonic acid . Hence, the induction of these proteins will prevent the release of arachidonic acid by phospholipase A2.
