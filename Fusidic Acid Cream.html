Fusidic acid is belongs to the antibiotic class and It is involved in the treatment of bacterial skin and eye infections. It provides relief from the small cuts and wounds that causes infection. It is derived from fungus.

  • Impetigo
  • Dermatitis
  • Boils
  • Bacterial conjunctivitis
  • Cellulitis

Fusidic acid acts by preventing the synthesis of proteins by inhibiting the translocation of elongation factor in the ribosome of nucleus. The proteins are required for the working of the vital function in bacteria therefore this further inhibits the multiplication and growth of bacteria.

Absorption- It is absorbed orally and shows bioavailability about 91%
Protein Binding- It is 97-99% bound to proteins
Metabolism- It undergoes decarboxylation, glucuronidation and glycation
Excretion- It excreted renally in very low levels
