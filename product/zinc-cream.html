DESCRIPTION

Zinc oxide ointment contains the active ingredient Zinc oxide. Zinc oxide ointment is used topically (for the skin) to treat diaper rash, minor burns, severely chapped skin, or other minor skin irritations.


Indications

  • TIt is used to treat or prevent diaper rash.
  • It is used to treat skin irritation.
  • It is used to treat poison oak, ivy, and sumac rashes.
  • It may be given to you for other reasons. Talk with the doctor.

How to use

  • Do not take this drug by mouth. Use on your skin only. Keep out of your mouth, nose, and eyes may burn
  • Wash your hands before and after use. If your hand is the treated area, do not wash your hand after use.
  • Clean affected parts before use. Make sure to dry well. Put on the affected part and rub gently.If you are using under a diaper, change the diaper often. Clean the affected area and allow it to dry.
  • Use the drug with each diaper changed, especially at bedtime.

Side Effects

Signs of an allergic reaction, like rash; hives; itching; red, swollen, blistered, or peeling skin with or without fever.


Warning & Precaution

  • Tell all of your health care providers that you take this drug. This includes your doctors, nurses, pharmacists, and dentists.
  • Do not use it more often or for longer than you were told.
  • This drug may stain clothing or fabric. Protect clothing and fabrics from staining.
  • This drug may cause harm if swallowed. If this drug is swallowed, call a doctor or poison control center right away.

Interactions

Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are taking, have recently taken or might take any other medicines. This includes any medicines which you have bought without a prescription or herbal medicines.


Contraindication

If you are allergic to this drug; any part of this drug; or any other drugs, foods, or substances. Tell your doctor about the allergy and what signs you had.


Storage

Store at room temperature in a dry place. Keep all drugs in a safe place. Keep all drugs out of the reach of children .
