Fluticasone & Imidurea Ointment

Indications:- Eczema & Psoriasis


Fluticasone & Imidurea Ointment

OINTMENT PRESENTATION:

Fluticasone Propionate IP 0.05% w/w
Imidurea USP 0.2% w/w

Fluticasone propionate is a synthetic glucocorticoid and is used topically for various inflammatory indications and itching caused by a number of skin conditions such as allergic reactions, eczema, and psoriasis.

  • Eczema
  • Psoriasis
  • Skin Inflammation

Fluticasone propionate activates glucocorticoid receptors, and inhibits lung eosinophilia. Fluticasone propionate as a topical formulation is also associated with vasoconstriction in the skin.
Imidurea is an antimicrobial preservative used in cosmetics. It acts as a formaldehyde releaser.

The risk or severity of edema formation can be increased when 16-Bromoepiandrosterone, 19-norandrostenedione is combined with Fluticasone propionate.

The cream is considered as safe when used in recommended doses however mild skin itching, burning, peeling, dryness, thinning or softening of skin may occur in some patients.

Not to be used in pregnancy and breast feeding.

  • Tell your doctor and pharmacist if you are allergic to fluticasone, formaldehyde, any other medications.
  • Tell your doctor and pharmacist what prescription and nonprescription medications, vitamins, and nutritional supplements you are taking or plan to take.
  • Avoid unnecessary or prolonged exposure to sunlight and to wear protective clothing, sunglasses, and sunscreen if you are using fluticasone lotion.
